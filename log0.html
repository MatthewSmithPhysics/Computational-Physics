Entry 0

Beginnings

I am currently a university student of maths and physics. On this programme, there are no assessments explicitly linked to computing beyond year 1. I have thus taken it upon myself to self-teach more advanced methods in computational physics and maths. This log will document my progress and also serves as evidence of any experience gained.

Going into this, I have five years of experience with programming and have completed one year of university education in maths and physics. I have completed courses named Analysis I, Calculus and Probability I, Linear Algebra I, Foundations of Physics 1, and Discovery Skills in Physics (labs). I thus have a fair base of knowledge in both computing and maths/physics. As for the interface of the two, I have a fair bit of experience with creating basic physics simulations, looking at topics such as projectile motion and double pendulums. I am also currently working on a basic linear algebra library in C++, named smithlin . I now want to tackle more advanced problems.

As of writing this, I have not acquired any textbooks to help me but I intend to soon buy one. I have seen reccommendations for Computational Physics by Thijssen, so I think I should get that. From the looks of things, it spans a good range of topics and so should be suitable. But for now, I can only wait.
